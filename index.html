Who I am

Hello! My name is Samuel Carr, a game designer / developer honing my skills. About a year ago, I graduated from Rochester Institute of Technology with a BS degree in Game Design and Development. In my game design and development I am focused on bringing fresh ideas and satisfying game play experiences using creativity, grit, and a vast range of skills. I am driven by a passion for creating quality gameplay and bringing new ideas to fruition. During my junior year of college, I completed a 6-month internship at Wayfair, where I learned many valuable skills and techniques and got a taste of working a full-time job at a professional company. Currently, I’m working as the lead designer / developer for a new company, Backwoods Gaming. We’ve been developing our first title, PANDO Engines, which is set to release a demo in a couple months.

My specialty when it comes to creating games is leading a team whilst focusing on satisfying gameplay and fresh ideas for game design. I led many teams while in college and currently with Backwoods Gaming, planning sprints, coming up with tasks / tickets, and providing direction in various game projects. My skills and experience span across industry standard applications like Photoshop, Maya, and Unity allow me to complete nearly every part of creating a game, from writing the scripts to implementing the assets.

During my 6-month internship as a software engineer at Wayfair, I was exposed to a large company tech stack, and worked directly with my team of skilled engineers as well as the entire engineering department to improve the Wayfair CLI. I learned new programming languages and skills that will be applicable to the current and future of the tech sector, like Git, containerization with Docker, and techniques for efficient CI. I worked directly specifically on making the testing pipeline for our engineering team run faster and more smoothly, by scanning the codebase for tests that were too slow or inefficient, and running diagnostics to find problems quickly, and creating visualizations that allowed the engineering team to quickly pinpoint problems as they cropped up.

Most recently I've been employed for the past 6 months by a startup game company, Backwoods Gaming, working on PANDO Engines, our debut title. With Backwoods I’ve acted as the lead game designer and have also been assisting our other developers with miscellaneous tasks, utilizing my wide breadth of game development skills to do things like creating the maps for the game, creating visual effects for the weather systems and weapons, scripting some of the hazards, programming UI, creating a player customization system, and creating a ping-based communication system. As for design work I’ve thus far created a detailed GDD (Game Design Document) that describes in detail game rules, diagrammed core gameplay features, defined the game loops and progression, and drafted UI interfaces. I’ve worked as the creative lead on the 6-person team, communicating between our producer, artist, developers, and CEO to make important decisions and plan for the future and constantly developing and iterating on the game’s concept, gameplay, and functionality.

Escalation

Worked as lead gameplay programmer and lead designer on a primary team of 3 programmers and 4 artists to create a demo version of Escalation, a 2.5D platformer where the player can jump, climb, and swing to the top of a skyscraper.

The illusion of rotation is created using a shader that rotates objects based on the camera’s position.

Worked as team lead, managing weekly sprints and team meetings, creating / assigning tasks from a backlog, and planning milestones and status reports every sprint. My specific contributions to the game were as follows:

  • Designed and coded player movement, including jumping, climbing, and swinging.
  • Created the rotation shader responsible for the rounded building effect.
  • Integrated animations for the player model.
  • Designed the tutorial level.

Link to YouTube playthrough: https://youtu.be/_QT427D9a1Q

Legend of the Leguminous Legion

Legend of the Leguminous Legion is a turn-based strategy game as it involves 4 players that take turns moving their pieces on the game board. The game involves strategy as players are challenged to earn more points than their opponents by out-maneuvering opponents’ pieces and planning to secure victory.

This project was a team effort in a group consisting of five people. My role was as the lead game designer. I pitched the idea and guided it through creation and playtesting.

Created a full design document, rule sheet, and physical prototype that was played at the school’s tabletop game club.

Frogue

Frogue is a fast-paced dungeon crawling experience in much the same vein as The Binding of Isaac and Enter the Gungeon. The game focuses on defeating swarms of enemies all while conserving water and and air to continue shooting without sustaining damage and falling in combat. Another primary focus will be that of slippery combat involving diving underneath the surface to carefully dodge obstacles.

I was the lead designer on the game, pitching the idea and leading the team's development. My specific accomplishments are as follows:

  • Acted as the lead designer for the game, influencing development, the design document, and creating the initial pitch
  • Coded player movement, including the major state machines for the game
  • Created a water shader for the swamp of the level and coded the diving mechanics
  • Implemented art assets for the player character and integrated the animations

Link to YouTube playthrough: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T89RqvrZ5aI
